Using the Rate Calculator

Example 1: ARS move - in CT - MD - going a total of 36.6 miles:
1. Choose your client (ARS)

2. Choose the State (CT)

3. Choose the weight class (MD)

4. Enter the loaded mileage (36.6) and click on the "Calculate" button to calculate TPG/ARS Rate as shown below.

5. Once you have a quote you would like to calculate overages on - fill in the VQ and ETA boxes.

6. Once you click "Check for Overages" - the program will calculate if any additional funds will be needed from ARS in order to proceed.
This is ONLY for fully covered vehicles, if the CX has a "partial coverage" - calculate the OOP MANUALLY.
In this case, the Vendor Quote is within ARS rates and within TP profit minimum. This will trigger the dispatch disclaimer and tell you to proceed with your dispatch as no additional funding is needed.
7. Lets say the VQ was $375 instead - it will kick back a red colored result showing $88.70 in additional funding needed to proceed.
As you can see, no dispatch disclaimer is populated therefore you are not authorized to proceed with the dispatch.
This will trigger a "FUNDS APPROVED" button directly under the amount.

8. Call ARS and ask for approval for these funds, once approved, hit "Funds Approved".
This will populate the "Additional Funds" template required to be noted in every move where additional was requested.
You can copy and paste the template into the move and add the name of the CSR approving the additional.
DO NOT FORGET TO UPDATE "CLIENT QUOTE" IN DETAILS PAGE AS WELL. Once complete, you can hit "Dispatch disclaimer" in order to dispatch your provider.

9. Dispatch your provider and update the customer & client with the ETA.

Example 2: AAA move - in FL - HD-RV - going a total of 45.4 miles:
1. Choose your client (AAA) and fill in the additional details needed to calculate.
This will only calculate for customers with a $500 RV Coverage.
If they are covered for mileage, you must calculate any overages MANUALLY.

2. Enter your vendor quote & ETA then click "Check for Overages" to calculate the OOP.

3. Once you have collected the OOP expense, click on "OOP Funds Collected" to populate the Note Template required for every move being dispatched.
Notate this in the move and continue to click on "Dispatch Disclaimer" to populate your disclaimer.

4. Dispatch your provider and update the customer with ETA.

Example 3: J&R Schugel move - in KS - T/T Combination - going a total of 136 miles:
1. Choose your client ("Other") and fill in the additional details needed to calculate.

2. Enter your vendor quote & ETA then click "Check for Overages" to populate your dispatch disclaimer.
If you are over TPG - follow instructions to get approval from your TEAM LEAD or Management before proceeding to dispatch.
