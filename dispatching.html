Truck & Trailer
Always ask if the trailer is loaded.
Split Tow states (truck and trailer have to be towed separate) will need a wrecker for each unit.
Port to Port means from the moment the truck leaves HQ to the moment they get back. Avoid hourly rates.


RVs / Travel Trailers
Always request photos from the customer. It is vital for us to be informed on the condition of these units to avoid a dry run.
Tires are an important factor in towing an RV/Trailer. If a unit has to be trailered, the cost will be substantially higher.
Ask if the unit is nosed in, or backed in to its parking spot. Also find out how about accesibility to the unit (does it have to be winched out of its spot and then loaded?)
Is the unit accessible? (is it in the driveway vs backyard) - A vendor will not enter the property to hook the unit if it is in the backyard and will charge for winching out of the backyard and into an accessible location.
