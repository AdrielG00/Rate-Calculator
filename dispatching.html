Gathering Quotes:
Vendors with higher move counts may give us better than pricing than others. It is usually good to start with those closest to the breakdown.
Split Tow states (truck and trailer have to be towed separate) will need a wrecker for each unit. Expect higher pricing and try to negotiate as best as possible.
Lead the conversation and get straight to the point. We need to keep these as brief as possible in order to assess our options - offer the pertinent information along with our price target early in.
Make sure you notate who you speak with and the equipment being used in the quote notes. (ie: "HD wrecker" "Rotator & Wrecker" "Split tow")
Consult with your team lead within the first 45 minutes of working your move with your best options and seek direction if needed.

If you are unable to find assistance within network:
1. Expand the mileage in your vendors to 100 miles - it is not unusual for a company to have a truck running that far out, especially if near a major city.
2. If the delivery location is a dealership - see if they have any recommendations in the area.
3. Use outside resources like truckdown and google in order sign up a new vendor who is able to assist.


Dispatching Moves:
Ensure that pricing is all in - taxes, tolls and credit card fees included.
Make sure to get approval from a team lead and collecting any out of pocket due before sending a move to a vendor.
*Vendor quote approval will be needed for quotes over TPG*
Make sure you are verbally dispatching the move information and confirming paperwork has been received.
Update quote notes with all important information. (ie: "Updated customer and email chain with eta" "Extended ETA w/ ARS on dispatch" "Client updated with service tomorrow")


Move Confirmation:
Make sure to notate who you spoke with and accurate times (if available).
Clients must be updated (when applicable) when units vendors are on scene, units are picked up & delivered.
Be sure to use the templates provided for communicating these updates.
