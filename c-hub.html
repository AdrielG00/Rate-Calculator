HUB group accident scene
• Client: Hub Group, Inc. – Accident Scene
• Email: Accidenttows@hubgroup.com
• TP alias: hubgroup@transitpros.com
• Intake: Email or Phone call. If intake is by phone, initiate an email at dispatch.

Communications:
 Intake: Email or Phone call. If intake is by phone, Initiate email to accidenttows@hubgroup.com Cc: hubgroup@transitpros.com

- Client updates: Email
 o Service Request Received
o ETA after dispatch
o On scene
o Picked up
o Delivered
o Any important updates in between

- Verify all details and unit condition with Hub driver
- Get photos if able
- Determine if police are on scene
o If no: Inform HUB Group driver we will have an ETA momentarily. Advise driver if PD does show up, let the officer know Transit Pros is sending a wrecker and to call TP immediately.
-Work FAST
-Have TP tow vendor take photos of unit and equipment on scene

o If yes: Inform TP is sending someone and confirm the police have not sent anyone. Speak to the officer directly if able
-Notify a manager or Senior CSR immediately
-If PD has already called a vendor out or there is a wrecker on scene: Find out who
-Call vendor and see if they will work out a quote to get the unit to client requested destination with TP directly. The goal here is to work out billing with the vendor and get the unit to the delivery if we have one. If vendor is refusing and taking unit to their lot – confirm with PD this was a nonconsensual tow.

If nonconsensual PD rotation tow:
• Update client immediately and let them know we will keep them updated
• Confirm the address of where the unit is being taken to
• Request the vendors invoice and photos of unit and vendor equipment on scene
ALWAYS request pictures in all accident scene scenarios: Pictures of unit and of the vendors equipment in use
* Nonconsensual towing: “Nonconsensual tow” is a police rotation call and runs on regulated tow / service rates establish between to vendor and the Police Dept. Transit Pros has no authority to call off a nonconsensual tow.
